Why would you want to track your daily life?

  • who will benefit and in what way from spending some time during the day tracking how they have spent their time
  • time management skill
  • good reflection practice

What time tracking is good for?

  • plan activities that can be achieved with some certainty when the \(n\) hours has been spend on that activity

certification 10000 hours rule :) great example, which I used is [learning a language](https://www.state.gov/foreign-language-training/) or mastering a [programing language](https://app.datacamp.com/learn/career-tracks/data-scientist-professional-with-r)

  • allows one to reflex in the moment if one has been spending his time productively;

  • gives you visual confirmation of how you spend your time;

ability to automate the process and provide instant feedback

  • knowing the value of one’s time (!)

  • time management

What time tracking is not good for?

  • tracking one’s productivity (!)

    you can infer this, but I’d advise against it, because you might fall victim to Goodheart’s Law - measure becomes a target. this should be simple nontaxing process of tracking

  • tracking many categories and subcategories

  • improving one’s life

    this is just a tool (not the only) to gain a perspective on your life, you should

My story (why have I started tracking my time?)

Sometime in Summer 2019 I was bored and wasted days doing a whole lot of nothing. Before I never knew one can get tired from doing nothing, but that summer I languished a lot.

On one such morning, having woken up at 12am hopelessly fatigue I had enough. I hated myself, for I was doing a whole lot of nothing, and at around that time I realized I needed meaning in my life - feeling of accomplishment and being proud of myself; was reading motivational books like 7 Habits and thinking about what do I do with my life. This is the WHY, now about the HOW.

Earlier, in winter 2019 I started low-key learning Japanese on Duolingo, Drops, you name it. Overtime I got exposure to some real-deal try-hard stuff: Anki-gang, AJATT/MIA/Migaku. Guys were hell bent of mastering the language, they would spend days with the language, cut their social ties with external world - I liked them. Their approach - spend as much time with the language seamed reasonable, after all this IS how you acquire language. Among some of the stuff the One of them shared a video of how he was tracking his Japanese learning journey. Stars aligned and I started tracking my time.
